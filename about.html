Our Story

The Notes of Ranvier are Johns Hopkins University’s FIRST co-ed community service a cappella group. We perform at various locations across Baltimore spreading good cheer and great a cappella! Our repertoire spans several seemingly discordant genres that we tie together with our trademark positivity. We bring a cappella to local schools and nursing homes, as well as the Johns Hopkins Children’s Hospital. We also like to improve and challenge ourselves when it comes to our art, and to that end, we participated in the International Collegiate Championships of A cappella (ICCA) for the first time in 2016. Much like the genres we cover, our members come from a variety of majors and backgrounds. Whenever we’re together (and not singing), you can hear simultaneous conversations on any number of subjects, forming another sort of harmony. Joining the Notes of Ranvier doesn’t just mean joining an a cappella group – it means discovering one of the strongest support systems you’ll find on campus, and finding mentors and fellow music lovers and friends. The heart of the Notes of Ranvier is our collective desire to bring a little bit of joy into the world, and we put this heart into every one of our performances.

Notes of Ranvier is Johns Hopkins' first service a cappella group!

Named after the unmylinated sheaths in the nervous system that allow us to feel & sing.
